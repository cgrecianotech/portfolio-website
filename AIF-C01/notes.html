About these Notes

These are comprehensive notes for the AWS AI Practitioner (AIF-C01) certification.

Sources

90% of the content is based on Stéphane Maarek's course (PAID, hosted in Udemy). Some content is based on Adrian Cantrill's Tech Fundamentals course (FREE, hosted in Teachable), his YouTube channel and his AWS Solution's Architect Associate course (PAID, hosted in Teachable). I highly encourage you to purchase Maarek's course in Udemy for video explanations and/or as additional reference. I also highly recommend doing Cantrill's Tech Fundamentals course (it's FREE) and, if you decide in the future to take the AWS Solution's Architect Associate (SAA-C03) certification, that you purchase Cantrill's course (it's a very high quality video course).

Preparing for the exam

Apart from taking these notes, I created ~300 Anki flashcards to review the material covered here. You can find my flashcards for FREE in this link: https://ankiweb.net/shared/info/73981918?cb=1729505948829 . I highly recommend to use my flashcards to review what you learn from Maarek's course and/or my notes. By studying at a steady pace and learning 20 new flashcards per day, you will cover the whole material in ~15 days. Once you cover the whole material, I highly suggest to do some practice exams. There are some free practice exams out there, but I also suggest buying some more: for example from Udemy or from Tutorials Dojo. Be careful not to use exam dumps! You could take 3-5 days to do practice exams, which means preparing for this certification in a total ~20 days is very realistic.

Shouldn't I be taking my own notes and creating my own flashcards?

Yes, if you can dedicate time to create your own notes and your own flashcards, by all means do so! They will be better for you than any other notes and flashcards you can find out there. However, the reality is that we are often bound by time constraints. I firmly believe that my notes and flashcards are of a generically high quality that can be useful for most people. It can also be a good idea to use a hybrid approach: use my notes and flashcards, and add some notes and flashcards of your own.
